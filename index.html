What is Mental Health?

Mental health refers to your emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

Recognizing Stress

Stress is a normal part of life, but when it becomes overwhelming, it can affect your mental health. Recognizing the signs of stress is the first step in managing it.

  • Feeling anxious or nervous.
  • Difficulty concentrating.
  • Feeling overwhelmed.
  • Headaches or muscle tension.

Managing Anxiety

Anxiety can be difficult to manage, but with the right strategies, it can be controlled. Here are some tips to help manage anxiety:

  • Practice deep breathing techniques.
  • Engage in regular physical activity.
  • Avoid caffeine and alcohol.
  • Talk to someone you trust about your feelings.

Support Resources

If you or someone you know is struggling with mental health, it's important to seek help. Below are some resources you can turn to:

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 0800-273-TALK
  • Therapists and Counselors: Visit NHS Psychiatry Information
  • Community Mental Health Support Groups: Find a Community Support Group or Organisation
